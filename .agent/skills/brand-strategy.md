# SKILL: Brand Strategy

**Trigger:** When @biz needs to define, refine, or audit the product's brand identity — voice, messaging, positioning, and guidelines for external communications.

---

## When to Use
- Defining brand identity for a new product or major rebrand.
- Creating messaging guidelines for marketing content.
- Auditing existing content for brand consistency.
- Preparing for external-facing launches or partnerships.
- Before any `content-marketing` or `seo-copywriting` task — ensure brand alignment.

---

## The Brand Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Brand Foundation
Define the core brand elements:

```markdown
## Brand Foundation — [Product Name]
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD | **Author:** @biz

### Mission
[One sentence: why this product exists]

### Vision
[One sentence: what the world looks like if we succeed]

### Values
1. **[Value 1]:** [What it means in practice]
2. **[Value 2]:** [What it means in practice]
3. **[Value 3]:** [What it means in practice]

### Target Audience
| Segment | Demographics | Psychographics | Primary Need |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Primary] | [Age, location, device] | [Attitudes, beliefs] | [What they want] |
| [Secondary] | [Age, location, device] | [Attitudes, beliefs] | [What they want] |
```

### Step 2: Positioning Statement
Use the classic positioning formula:

```
For [target audience]
who [need/want/frustration],
[Product Name] is a [category]
that [key benefit/differentiator].
Unlike [primary alternative],
we [unique advantage].
```

**Example:** *For Vietnamese people who check lunar dates and seek fortune guidance, Lịch Việt is an all-in-one metaphysics platform that combines academic-grade astrology with a beautiful, mobile-first experience. Unlike paper almanacs or basic calendar apps, we offer 11 interconnected divination engines with professional-depth analysis.*

### Step 3: Brand Voice
Define how the brand speaks:

| Dimension | We Are | We Are NOT |
|---|---|---|
| **Tone** | [Warm, knowledgeable, respectful] | [Cold, condescending, casual] |
| **Language** | [Vietnamese-first, clear, accessible] | [Jargon-heavy, English-dominant] |
| **Expertise** | [Academic, well-researched, trustworthy] | [Pop astrology, vague, unverified] |
| **Personality** | [Modern sage — tradition meets technology] | [Mystical guru or boring almanac] |

**Voice Examples:**
| Context | On-Brand | Off-Brand |
|---|---|---|
| Feature announcement | "Khám phá lá số Tử Vi chính xác theo trường phái Tam Hợp" | "Check your horoscope now!" |
| Error message | "Chưa thể tải dữ liệu. Vui lòng thử lại sau." | "Error 500" |
| Marketing headline | "Hiểu vận mệnh, sống chủ động" | "The #1 Vietnamese astrology app" |
| Social post | "Hôm nay là ngày Hoàng Đạo — thích hợp khởi sự, giao dịch " | "OMG today is a lucky day!! " |

## Rules
- **Consistency > creativity.** A consistent brand builds trust faster than a clever one-off.
- **Vietnamese-first, always.** The primary audience speaks Vietnamese. Brand voice must feel natural in Vietnamese.
- **Evolve, don't pivot.** Brand updates should be evolutionary, not revolutionary (unless rebranding).
- **Every team member is a brand ambassador.** @designer implements the visual brand; @biz defines the voice; everyone upholds it.
- **Audit quarterly.** Brand drift is real — check touchpoints every quarter.
